India's Comeback

Advanced Manufacturing

On a bigger note, standards of living are elevated by the manufacturing sector. It helps in contributing a larger scale for the overall economy. It is an all time working sector which plays an important role considering our existence. But the sector has faced a major fall down as the covid situation never gave space for manpower. Coming back to its usual rate and to cope up with the needs, it is mandatory for innovations to enhance the labour.

While workers are back in form after having a longer break, it is important to give them external power through innovations . Also it is significant to bring up the small scale industries and to set up a smart manufacturing unit. Collectively the unit is in need of mass workers but with overcoming the challenge of social distancing. These are the areas that needs technical assistance to be worked on.

Economies all over the world are ramping back up . The post covid scenario has presented several unique challenges to the manufacturing sector. Billions of people around the globe are dependent on resilient supply chains and timely delivery of essential products. With the need for the goods on the rise , the requirement of employees on the field have also surged up. This raises a serious issue , that is to ensure that goods being produced are manufactured under safe and sanitized conditions when delivered to the consumers. This calls for the development of a system that curbs such challenges and helps us mitigate this tough time with smart solutions.
The pandemic has affected all sectors of the society and amongst the major ones affected were the manufacturing lines. It has elevated some serious issues and the predominant one is the labour procuring , especially for small scale industries, as the labourers are mostly sourced from adjoining states.With industries fastly picking up pace the labour and resource availability are not on the same track. The outbreak has caused a large amount of temporary labour to migrate to various places, mainly hometowns, to settle for another occupation. Thus labour shortage is hitting both industrial and non industrial sectors. While some industries are facing this issue , few labourers are unable to return citing various reasons , and this decreased the labour engaging rate drastically down the graph. Thus solutions addressing these problems are welcomed widely.
Despite considerable hype since the last few years, advanced factory automation has not been able to substitute at large scale as shown by COVID 19. This pandemic may and should serve to accelerate the adoption of smart technologies and methodologies fostering smart manufacturing. Companies are gradually realizing the convenience of machine to machine communication and connectivity across the value chain, especially remote monitoring and real-time control of the manufacturing shop floor. With the gradual uptake of smart automation in many companies, there's an unprecedented complexity of safety risks exposed to the operators of the production facilities. The traditional safety assessments are now inadequate. Concept of smart safety arises to handle the new challenges. Real-time safety assessments, Interoperability validation, cyber security and functional safety becomes the new hygiene factors. Providing alternate solutions or systems and ensuring work safety at the same time for such industries will move businesses and the economy in a better way.

Health care

While the concern of the whole world is associated with health, we have various challenges in the area of health care. Recovering back from the pandemic situation not only involves in increasing the recovery rate but it also includes in taking care of health expanse. Since we are in the mid of contagious diseases, non contact and virtual hospital system should be in charge. Detecting illness in a safer manner is also needed.

So as to achieve the requisite, technology has to play an important role. Medication and treatment in the pandemic situation is a salient obligation but making it safer and contactless is in the hands of technology. Be it pandemic or not, hygiene in a public environment is indeed a basic thing. Hence solutions can be brought up for all the existing problems.

Virtual health is a key component of our future of health vision. Virtual health has the capacity to inform, personalize, accelerate, and augment prevention and care. And now with the ongoing post pandemic scenario , going contactless is paramount . Health care sector stands as the frontline in saving people almost everyday , safety and hygiene in such a field cant be compromised. The best way to approach healthcare in such situations is to develop a virtual health care system. Virtual health also gives health stakeholders patients the ability to share data and content and perform personalized interactions remotely. This can allow for convenient, high-quality access to care that can enhance provider-patient interactions. In addition, virtual health-facilitated robotics and automation can help relieve clinicians of mundane, administrative, or routine tasks, giving them more opportunities to practice at the top of their license. Thus any system fostering virtual health care would serve as a boon for the future of this sector.
Wearing a face mask doesn’t guarantee complete safety from viruses/bacteria, as they can also be transmitted through the eyes or nose as tiny viral particles known as aerosols. However, masks are effective at capturing droplets, which is identified as the main mode of transmission for coronavirus, and some studies have estimated that roughly fivefold protection is needed to curb the spread. Disposal of these masks is also uncontrolled today. With people getting back to normal, masks are critical especially for healthcare professionals, the elderly, the affected individuals, individuals who are taking care of the infected and to those who are in close proximity to the infected. As their availability is scarce and limited, it calls for a possible solution and design of washable and reusable masks and as they are demanded by people of different economic backgrounds, they should be made affordable and reusable, as it is proven that detergents and soaps kill the protein fats of the virus cells and make them ineffective.
Though 90% of the people have resumed back to work and vaccine doses are being given , hygiene still holds its place in preventing another major outbreak of the virus. This calls for the hygiene of the hospitals to be taken seriously. Keeping hospitals clean is a crucial patient safety issue. The importance of the hospital environment in patient care has only recently been recognized widely in infection prevention and control (IPC). Although the available literature is limited, there is enough evidence to demonstrate that cleaning hospitals helps prevent infections. Thus advanced tech solutions to ensure hygiene of hospitals are in need of the hour to mitigate the challenging situation.

Smart city

The word 'smart' has taken us over in the past decade. Smart works are merely appreciated because they involve less effort and are more feasible. Also when it comes to the process of recovering back, smart projects play a major role. Energy saving being its utmost concern and making things available in a better way is the motive of smart plans. For example, in the near future we may have to face elections which call for the crowd , we must find solutions to progress the elections. Keeping in mind all such situations, hoping to witness more smart projects.

Physical distancing, as a measure to contain the spreading of Covid-19, is defining a “new normal”.Crowd management is a challenging scientific topic directly impacting on the functioning of trafficked urban infrastructures such as, e.g train or metro stations. Unless belonging to a family, pedestrians in shared spaces are asked to observe a minimal (country-dependent) pairwise distance. Coherently, managers of public spaces may be tasked with the enforcement or monitoring of this constraint.
With the economies getting back to normal it's high time we think about viable and efficient solutions to overcome these challenges. A system to showcase stock of supplies and indicate crowd at local stores in prior would help reduce unnecessary crowding at the stores and be helpful in managing resources effectively.
The term “contactless” has become the key component for normalizing almost all sectors of the economy. With people commuting back to work , doors are one of the most touched surfaces by multiple people in hospitals, offices, and other places thus making it prone to virus infection. In order to avoid this, an automated system for opening and closing doors can be put in place, by using IoT technology. Also by AI based systems, entry restrictions can be put forth, say for example by identifying the people who have a high temperature.
One of the cascading effects of COVID - 19 pandemic has been its impact on democracies.There’s never been a postponement of a national election. Our democratic process has been upheld during world wars and civil wars. The question at hand is, how do we ensure our democratic process stays intact knowing the pandemic has shifted our hourly, daily, and weekly routines? A methodology or a system to curb the challenges to be faced during election times as we slowly rise up from the pandemic will necessarily ease the process of voting and benefit the safety of the citizens.

Future mobility

The Indian automotive industry has started to experience the effects of the global disruption. Transportations is always a key for productivity of people or even a city. But with unexpected rises in gasoline prices, climate change and the effects of pandemic’s still lingering, there is a pressing need for immediate innovations and changes. The future awaits with various problems in mobility until the country runs completely electric. Electrification is of particular importance, and might significantly impact auto component manufacturers. By laying down a foundation of support, innovating on Technologies (eg, batteries, swapping infrastructure, fast chargers, economic alternate vehicles, etc.) we can ease the adoption. Apart from electrification, mobility also involves other factors like congestion, emissions, etc that are a problem, finding smart and innovative solutions are necessary to avoid future issues.

As cities reopen and look to live safely and sustainably with the virus, there is an urgent need to act quickly to ensure they don’t go from lockdown to gridlock. Past pandemics and early experience of COVID-19 clearly indicate that, without investment, cities can expect a significant shift from public transit to private cars as lockdown restrictions ease. Until the virus is gone, there remains a risk that large numbers of people using public transport could lead to a rise in COVID-19 transmission. To reduce this risk, there is a need to introduce or maintain a raft of measures already implemented in cities that have seen outbreaks. This will make public transport safer for staff and the individuals using it, reduce community transmission and help to restore trust in public transport for the longer term.
The lockdown certainly impacted the on road mobility of vehicles on a large scale . It's been almost a year since the transportation has seen normal commuting rates. But now with the relaxation of lockdown it is expected that the transportation will be back to normal and undoubtedly the traffic rates will also surge up. Therefore it is essential that we come up with smart solutions to ease congestion and facilitate better transport.
If this covid had taught us one thing , then it's definitely the conservation of the resources . It's high time we all take steps to stop exploiting the resources and find better and environment friendly solutions. On this note , one major thing to address is the consumption of gasoline in India. In India, the transport sector alone accounts for 18% of the total energy consumption. This sector will require approximately 200 million tonnes of oil equivalent of energy supply annually by 2030 if the current consumption trend continues. An economical and an eco friendly approach for this would be the introduction of electric mobility. Electric mobility, coupled with affordable technology and support infrastructure, is a possible alternative to tackle this challenge.

Education

The pandemic letting its damage on every single area did not even exclude education. People who prefer soft copies over hard copies are having a good time. Online classes are a threat to students. Online classes lack effectiveness and fail to connect with the students. We are in the urge of finding out an innovative solution comprising both online and offline modes for the betterment of students. Also we are in the need of educating the students to handle such situations in the near future. Considering the challenge put before, solutions are appreciated.

The post pandemic scenario poses several challenges in all walks of society including education. In order to keep education running, educational institutions have had to quickly adapt to the situation. With educational institutions functioning back to normal, intelligent collective actions need to be taken to ensure the safety of the students. Precautionary measures which include complete sanitation , social distancing, contactless classroom culture have to be enforced blended with advanced ed - tech concepts to bring in a progressive approach to education.
The Covid-19 pandemic raging around the globe has caused large-scale institutional and behavioural ‘shock effects’ in various areas of human activity including education. What most of us consider normal has already fundamentally shifted. The classroom culture took a rapid change towards getting digitized. While students are having a tough time coping up with lectures online , it also brings in serious health issues. Even after pandemic is over it is predicted that 40% of the classes will happen from home. This brings us to the big picture that we are in need of ed tech solutions to ensure efficient lecture delivery, by not compromising student’s health also.
